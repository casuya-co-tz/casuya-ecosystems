Solving Quadratic Equations via the Algebraic Formula

Interactive Step-by-Step Derivation
Given the second-degree polynomial equation: $$\mathbf{2x^2 + 3x - 2 = 0}$$ Find all real values of $x$ that satisfy the proposition.
Step 0 / 7
Step 1: Identify Coefficients
Map the given equation to the standard quadratic form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$. By standard comparison, we extract the structural coefficients:
$a = 2, \quad b = 3, \quad c = -2$
Step 2: State the Quadratic Formula
By the method of completing the square, the generic solutions for any quadratic equation are given by the Quadratic Formula:
$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$
Step 3: Algebraic Substitution
Substitute the mapped coefficients into the structural framework of the formula:
$$x = \frac{-(3) \pm \sqrt{(3)^2 - 4(2)(-2)}}{2(2)}$$
Step 4: Evaluate the Discriminant
Isolate and evaluate the radicand argument, known as the discriminant $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$:
$$\Delta = 3^2 - 4(2)(-2) = 9 - (-16) = 9 + 16 = 25$$
Since $\Delta > 0$, the equation yields two distinct real roots.
Step 5: Radical Simplification
Reintroduce the evaluated discriminant back into the radical expression:
$$x = \frac{-3 \pm \sqrt{25}}{4}$$
Evaluating the perfect square yields $\sqrt{25} = 5$:
$$x = \frac{-3 \pm 5}{4}$$
Step 6: Branch Solution Space
Partition the equation into its two canonical solution branches:
$$x_1 = \frac{-3 + 5}{4} = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}$$
$$x_2 = \frac{-3 - 5}{4} = \frac{-8}{4} = -2$$
Final Solution Set
The solution set $S$ containing the valid roots for the quadratic function is:
$$\mathbf{S = \left\{ \frac{1}{2}, \; -2 \right\}}$$
